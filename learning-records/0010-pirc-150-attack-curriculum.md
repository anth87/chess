# LR-0010 — Pirc 150 Attack: O-O · c5 mantra understood

**Date:** 2026-06-21
**Session type:** Lesson delivery (Lesson 10)
**ZPD position:** Already knows Pirc Classical (LR-0002) and Pirc Geller (LR-0008). This lesson closes the third major White system against the Pirc.

## What was demonstrated / covered

- **Pirc 150 Attack line**: 1.e4 d6 2.d4 Nf6 3.Nc3 g6 4.Be3 Bg7 5.Qd2
- **Correct response**: 5…O-O followed immediately by 6…c5 (after 6.O-O-O). The lever …c5 attacks d4 before White can play f3/g4/h4.
- **Recurring mistake pattern**: 5…Ng4 — the knight has no future on g4, White retreats the bishop (Bg5/Bf4), Black pushes …h6 and …g5 to chase it, weakening the kingside for the rest of the game.
- **Universal Pirc rule**: Never voluntarily weaken the Bg7 pawn cover or trade the g7 bishop without concrete gain. The bishop controls d4/e5 and is Black's most important piece.
