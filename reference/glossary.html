Alekhine Glossary

Canonical terms for this teaching workspace (White vs Alekhine).

Opening structure

Normal Variation
The main line after 1.e4 Nf6 2.e5 Nd5 3.d4 d6 4.Nf3 — White develops naturally while holding the e5+d4 center.

Avoid: calling every Alekhine game "the Normal" without the 4.Nf3 branch.

Big center
White's expanded pawn formation (e5, d4, and sometimes c4) that Black aims to attack and undermine rather than occupy.

Avoid: "huge pawn wall" — imprecise; the pawns can become targets.

Hypermodern
An opening philosophy where one side controls the center with pieces from afar, inviting the opponent's pawns forward to attack them later.

Key positions

Bg4 pin
Black's 4…Bg4, pinning the f3-knight to the queen — forcing White to commit to Be2, h3, or accept …Bxf3.
Doubled f-pawns
After …Bxf3 gxf3, White's kingside pawn structure is fractured — giving Black long-term targets on f3/f4.

Avoid: treating doubled f-pawns as always losing — White often gets the bishop pair in return.

e5 anchor
White's advanced e5-pawn that gives space and restricts …e5, but requires constant defense against …f6 and piece pressure.
