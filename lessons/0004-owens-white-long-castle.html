Owen's Defense: Long Castle First

~10 min · One tangible win: a 4-step setup vs 1…b6 without burning tempo

Why this lesson exists

Your leak map flags Owen's Defense (1…b6) as a top White loss cluster — offbeat, but you face it often enough that guessing costs rating. In your win vs ssharipov you castled long by move 6 and steamrolled the kingside. In your loss vs jirias you played 4.a3 before Black had pinned on c3, lost a tempo, and never got the same grip.

Your 4-step mantra (3+0)

Be3 · Qd2 · O-O-O · Bb5. Develop the dark-squared bishop, connect rooks with Qd2, castle long by move 6–7, then pin with Bb5. Only play a3 if Black has played …Bb4.

The setup that worked — your win

1.e4 b6 2.d4 Bb7 3.Nc3 e6 4.Be3 d6 5.Qd2 Qd7 6.O-O-O

Position from your win vs ssharipov · 2026-03-02 · verified via chess.js

Black's bishop on b7 is hemmed in by your e-pawn — it rarely becomes active. Your job is to finish development and attack before Black untangles with …Be7 and …Nf6. You followed with 7.Bb5, then f4, e5, and a kingside pawn storm that won on the clock.

What went wrong — your loss vs jirias

Same start, different fourth move: 4.a3 instead of 4.Be3. Black had played 4…Be7, not …Bb4 — so a3 did not address any threat. You gave Black a free tempo to finish …d6, …Nd7, and …c5 with less pressure.

Position from your loss vs jirias · 2026-05-29 · verified via chess.js

The rule: a3 only answers …Bb4. Otherwise Be3 immediately, Qd2, O-O-O — the same rhythm that beat ssharipov.
