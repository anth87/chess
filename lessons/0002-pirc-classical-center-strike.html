Your 5-step mantra (3+0)

Castle · Nbd7 · take on e5 · Ng4 · c5. Fianchetto and castle first. When White pushes e5, take …dxe5. Jump the knight to g4 to attack e5. Then play …c5 to hit d4 — that is your counterplay.

What White does next

White's main try is 9.e6, chasing the f7 pawn. You answered with 9…Nc5 — fine. After 10.exf7+ Kh8 11.Bg5 you played 11…Bxc3, traded queens, and sank into a worse rook endgame.

At 11, your knight already sits on c5 (from 9…Nc5) — a pawn cannot also land there. Play 11…h6 to chase the bishop, not 11…Bxc3.
