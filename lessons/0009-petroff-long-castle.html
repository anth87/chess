Petroff Classical: Long Castle First

~10 min · One tangible win: castle long and roll the kingside before Black equalises

The 3-step mantra (after 5.Nc3 Nxc3 6.dxc3)

Bf4 · Qd2 · O-O-O. Develop the bishop first — before choosing your castle side — then connect the rooks with Qd2, then castle long. King to c1, rook to d1 on the open file. The kingside storm (h4 · g4 · h5) follows naturally. Never play O-O here: short castle lets Black equalise without a fight.

The decision point — your move 7

After 5.Nc3 Nxc3 6.dxc3 Be7, the position looks quiet. White has doubled c-pawns but an open d-file and a bishop pair. The key is to develop Bf4 before Black can neutralise your light-squared bishop. Do not play Bc4 here — it commits you to short castle and the advantage evaporates.

The win — long castle secured

7.Bf4 O-O 8.Qd2 Nc6 9.O-O-O

The pitfall — short castle costs half a point

In a related 3…Nc6 Petroff line (January 2026), you tried Bc4 then O-O (short castle). The position remained level throughout — Black equalised comfortably, and the game was drawn by agreement on move 40. Short castle signals to Black that you have no ambition; long castle signals an attack.

Rule: Bf4 before Bc4. Qd2 before O-O. The long castle gives you the d-file and a safe king; the short castle gives Black what they want — equality.
