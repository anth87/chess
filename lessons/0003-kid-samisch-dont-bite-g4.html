Your 4-step mantra (3+0)

…e5 · …Nc5 · …h6 · …b5. When White pushes g4, strike the centre with …e5 — not …Nxg4. After d5, jump …Nc5. When White plays h5, blunt with …h6. Queenside …b5 only after the centre is stable.

What went wrong at move 11

White played 11.h5. You answered 11…Nxg4?? — capturing the pawn opens the f-file, lets White recapture with 12.fxg4, and brings the dark-squared bishop to g4 with tempo. You traded the initiative you earned with …e5 for a passive kingside.

Play 11…h6 instead. You keep the knight, blunt h5, and can still chase the bishop with …g5 later if needed.

After your mistake, the game continued 12.fxg4 Bxg4 13.Be2 Bxe2 14.Ngxe2 b5 — queenside play too late while White's pieces flooded the kingside. You were checkmated on move 36.
