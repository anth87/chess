Berlin / Zukertort: Centre First

~10 min · One tangible win: a 5-step setup vs 4…Bc5 without Bg5 traps

Your 5-step mantra (3+0)

c3 · Re1 · h3 · d4 · Nc3. Stabilize with c3, pressure e5 with Re1, stop …Bg4 with h3, then open the centre with d4. Develop Nc3 before Bg5 when …Nxd4 is possible.

The setup that worked — your win

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bb5 Nf6 4.O-O Bc5 5.c3 O-O 6.Re1 d6 7.h3 h6

You followed with 8.d4 exd4 9.cxd4 Bb6 10.Bxc6, opened the e-file, and won material on e5 — the patient setup paid off before any tactics.

What went wrong — your loss vs eggyteddy

Same Zukertort start, but after 9…Bd7 you played 10.Bg5 and 11.Bh4 without Nc3. Black's knight jumped to d4, forking the bishop on h4 and the knight on f3. You traded into a lost endgame instead of keeping the initiative.

Position from your loss vs eggyteddy · 2026-02-22 · verified via chess.js

The rule: Nc3 before Bg5 when the d4 pawn is fixed and …Nxd4 is possible. Finish c3 · Re1 · h3 · d4 first — the same rhythm that beat francescwow.
