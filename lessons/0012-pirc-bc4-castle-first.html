Your 3-step mantra (3+0)

g6 · Bg7 · O-O. Fianchetto, then castle on move 5 when White plays 5.Bg5. Do not play …c6 first — it blocks your bishop and leaves the king in the centre. Save …c5 for later (same lever as Lessons 2, 8, 10). Never chase Bg5 with …h6/…g5.

Mistake #1 — …c6 before castling

In turay24 and asifanwaruk you played 5…c6 (or 6…c6) while the king still sat on e8. …c6 looks solid but it blocks your Bg7 from influencing d4/e5 and delays castling. White finishes development with tempo while your king is stuck.

Mistake #2 — chasing Bg5 with …h6/…g5

When White plays 3.Bxf7+ (the Fried Liver trick), taking on f7 is fine — but then you must develop fast with …Nc6 and …Bg7. In your loss vs kareem702 you chased the bishop with …h6 and …g5. White kept the pair, opened the h-file, and your king never found safety.
